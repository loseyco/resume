About Me

Electronics Engineer across medical equipment, professional racing, and IT systems. Currently trackside engineer for Honda in IndyCar. I work effectively as team leader or solo, comfortable in corporate environments or hands-on field work.

I was the first engineer on-site building the Proton Accelerator at OSF Cancer Center in Peoria, IL. I led the complete installation from project initiation through first patient treatment, managing teams, configuring IT infrastructure, and ensuring system readiness for clinical operation.

In racing, I've served as trackside engineer, data strategist, and team manager for world-class teams including Honda Racing Corporation. My path started in flat track racing, led to founding iRacersResource and the ISRA League (one of the largest early iRacing leagues), then to professional road racing in 2011 when RP Motorsport brought me trackside at St. Petersburg.

I code daily using React, Supabase, Vercel, and automation platforms. Since 2002, I've freelanced through Losey.Co providing web development, IT services, and race team consulting.
